Surveys & schemes / DTES

When it started

DTES is being run as part of the NSS 80th Round, the latest in a series of tourism expenditure surveys conducted periodically to size and understand India's domestic tourism economy.

Motive of the survey

Tourism contributes heavily to states like Himachal Pradesh, but most of that spending happens outside any tax or business record that gets aggregated nationally. DTES exists to estimate that spend directly from travellers and households, giving planners a state-level picture of tourism's economic footprint.

What is analysed

Trip expenditureAmount spent per trip, broken into categories.
Purpose of visitLeisure, business, pilgrimage, or other reasons for travel.
Mode of travel & stayTransport used and type of accommodation.
Trip durationNumber of nights spent away from home.
Origin & destinationWhere travellers come from and where they go.
Household tourism spendAnnual travel spending patterns by household.

Field operation in Himachal Pradesh

First Stage Unit data is currently being submitted from the field and compiled into a state-level data room, with destination-state decoding to separate in-state and out-of-state tourist trips.
