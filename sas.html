When it started

SAS has been run in successive NSS rounds, most recently as part of the 77th Round, to update the picture of farm household well-being between full agriculture censuses.

Motive of the survey

Crop production data alone doesn't say whether the households growing that crop are financially secure. SAS asks farm households directly about income, spending and debt, so policy on farm support and rural credit is based on their actual situation, not just output figures.

What is analysed

Household incomeFrom cultivation, livestock, wages and other sources.
Farming practicesLand holding size, crop choices, input use.
IndebtednessLoans taken and their source.
Access to schemesAwareness and use of government farm support programmes.
