When it started

UFS has been conducted in recurring cycles since the 1970s, re-surveyed roughly every decade to keep pace with urban growth. Towns and cities are re-divided into enumeration blocks each cycle, since the last frame goes out of date as new construction and boundary changes reshape urban areas.

Motive of the survey

UFS does not measure a topic directly — it builds the map that every other urban survey depends on. Without an up-to-date block frame, samples drawn for PLFS, DTES or any household survey in a town would be biased toward older, already-mapped areas.

What is analysed

Block boundariesPhysical division of a town into enumerable units.
Household countsNumber of households per block, for sampling weight.
Land useResidential, commercial and mixed-use areas.
Growth & changeNew construction and boundary shifts since the last cycle.

Field operation in Himachal Pradesh

Block scrutiny is currently underway for Una town, using the official scrutiny checklist to verify block boundaries and household counts against the previous frame.
