Surveys & schemes / PLFS

When it started

PLFS was launched in April 2017, replacing the earlier system of quinquennial (five-yearly) employment-unemployment surveys. It introduced, for the first time, quarterly employment estimates for urban areas alongside annual estimates covering both rural and urban India.

Motive of the survey

The survey exists to give policymakers a timely read on India's labour market — something the old five-year cycle couldn't provide. It tracks how many people are working, looking for work, or outside the workforce, and how that changes quarter to quarter in cities and year to year across the country.

What is analysed

Labour Force Participation RateShare of the population working or seeking work.
Worker Population RatioShare of the population that is employed.
Unemployment RateShare of the labour force without work but seeking it.
Industry & occupationWhat sector and role each worker is engaged in.
Nature of employmentRegular wage, self-employed, or casual labour.
EarningsAverage wages and incomes by employment type.

Field operation in Himachal Pradesh

Currently in Panel 5 of the rotational sample, with Field Officers visiting First Stage Units across all 12 districts. Progress is tracked centre-sample by centre-sample through this office's field management system.
